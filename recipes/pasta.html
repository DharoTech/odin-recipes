Pasta Recipe




Description

Pasta is a beloved staple of Italian cuisine, celebrated for its versatility and comforting flavors. Made from simple ingredients like flour, eggs, and water, pasta can be paired with endless sauces — from rich tomato and creamy Alfredo to light olive oil and herbs. Whether served as a quick weeknight meal or part of a special dinner, a homemade pasta dish brings warmth, flavor, and tradition to the table.


Ingredients

  • 12 ounces pasta (spaghetti, penne, or your favorite type)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (14-ounce) can diced tomatoes or 2 cups fresh chopped tomatoes
  • 1 teaspoon dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon dried oregano
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional)
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Fresh basil leaves (for garnish)

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the pasta and cook according to the package instructions until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 30 seconds, until fragrant.
  3. Add the diced tomatoes, basil, oregano, salt, pepper, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir well and let simmer for 10–15 minutes to develop flavor.
  4. Add the cooked pasta to the skillet and toss until evenly coated with the sauce.
  5. Sprinkle with Parmesan cheese and stir gently until the cheese melts into the sauce.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with fresh basil leaves and an extra sprinkle of Parmesan if desired.
