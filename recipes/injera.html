Injera Recipe





Description

Injera is a traditional Ethiopian and Eritrean flatbread known for its unique spongy texture and slightly tangy flavor. Made primarily from teff flour—a gluten-free, nutrient-rich grain—injera serves as both a plate and utensil in East African cuisine. Its porous surface is perfect for soaking up flavorful stews like doro wat and misir wat, making every bite a delicious combination of texture and taste. Fermented for a few days to develop its signature sourness, injera is as much a cultural staple as it is a versatile and healthy accompaniment to many traditional dishes.


Ingredients

  • 2 cups teff flour (or a mix of teff and all-purpose flour)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1/4 teaspoon salt
  • Oil or cooking spray (for the pan)
  • Optional: a small amount of starter or yeast to aid fermentation

Directions

  1. In a large bowl, combine the teff flour and water. Stir until smooth and lump-free.
  2. Cover the bowl with a clean cloth and let it sit at room temperature for 2–3 days to ferment. You’ll know it’s ready when it smells slightly sour and bubbles form on the surface.
  3. Once fermented, gently stir in the salt.
  4. Preheat a non-stick skillet or specialized injera pan over medium heat. Lightly grease it with oil or cooking spray.
  5. Pour a thin layer of the batter onto the pan, swirling it quickly to cover the surface evenly.
  6. Cover and cook for 1–2 minutes until the surface is set and filled with bubbles. Do not flip.
  7. Carefully remove the injera and let it cool on a clean cloth. Repeat with the remaining batter.
  8. Serve warm with traditional Ethiopian stews or curries.
